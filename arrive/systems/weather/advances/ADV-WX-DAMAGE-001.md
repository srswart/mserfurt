## Objective

Implement targeted physical damage effects derived from CLIO-7 historical analysis: water damage confined to folios f04r-f05v with tide line generation via diffusion-limited aggregation, and a missing corner on f04v with a random-walk tear path along vellum grain.

## Behavioral Change

After this advance:
- Water damage applies only to folios f04r, f04v, f05r, and f05v with a top-down gradient (direction: from_above), producing ink dissolution at 0.4 intensity and vellum darkening at 0.12 intensity, with a naturalistic tide line boundary generated by diffusion-limited aggregation
- Missing corner on f04v removes a bottom-right region of approximately 35mm x 28mm, with the tear edge following a random walk along vellum grain direction at 0.65 irregularity, filled with backing board color RGB [45, 42, 38]
- All other folios pass through the damage stage unmodified

## Risk + Rollback

**Risks:**
- DLA tide line computation may be expensive for high-resolution page images; may need spatial downsampling and upscaling
- Random walk tear path must respect page boundaries to avoid removing content outside the corner region
- Damage rendering contract (public API) must be stable since groundtruth depends on exact damage zone masks

**Rollback:**
- Revert the feat/weather-damage-init branch; compositor skips damage layer and passes substrate+ink through to aging
